# Landing

## What it is
A single, minimal landing page. Exactly one page. No extra pages, routes, sections, or features.

## Users
General web visitors arriving from a shared link, ad, or social post — prospective customers/leads who need a quick, clear pitch and one obvious next step.

## The one job
Present a clean hero: a clear headline, a short supporting pitch line, and ONE primary call-to-action button. Nothing else.

## Tone & brand
Clean, professional, neutral. No specific product/offer given yet, so use tasteful placeholder copy that is easy to repoint (generic headline, subhead, CTA label like "Get Started"). Keep branding light and neutral.

## Strategic principles
- STRICT minimalism — the product decision, not a placeholder for "more later."
- Exactly ONE page. No nav, no multiple sections beyond the single hero, no footer link clusters, no secondary pages, no feature the user did not ask for.
- Ship fast and clean; make headline/pitch/CTA copy trivially easy to swap later.

## Anti-references
No multi-section marketing sites, no nav bars, no pricing tables, no forms beyond the single CTA, no blog, no dashboard.
